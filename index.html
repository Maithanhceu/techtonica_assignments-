Mai's King Cake Recipe

One of my all-time favorite foods from New Orleans is King Cake, especially during Mardi Gras season. It's a delicious cinnamon roll cake with various fillings and topped with icing. Traditionally, a tiny toy baby is hidden inside the cake, and whoever finds it has to buy the next King Cake.

The most popular King Cake in the area is from Dong Phoung, a James Beard-awarded establishment that is famous for its traditional Vietnamese dishes like Pho and Banh mi. Dong Phuong holds a special place in the hearts of New Orleans-East residents. It's where my mother worked as a waitress when she first came to the US, and the food she brought home from the restaurant became a staple in our household and throughout the community.

Ingredients

    For the Dough:

  • 1 cup warm milk (110째F/45째C)
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 5 egg yolks, beaten
  • 2 tablespoons active dry yeast
  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

For the Filling:

  • 1 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon
  • 2/3 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • Optional: 1 cup chopped pecans or walnuts, or 1/2 cup raisins
  • 2 tablespoons active dry yeast

    • For the Icing:

  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

    • For Decoration:

  • Purple, green, and gold (yellow) colored sugars
  • Optional: A small plastic baby, to be hidden inside the cake

    • Steps to Make King Cake

    Prepare the Dough:

    1. In a large bowl, combine the warm milk, sugar, and yeast. Let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy.
    2. Add the melted butter, beaten egg yolks, flour, salt, nutmeg, and cinnamon. Mix until a dough forms.
    3. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for about 10 minutes until it is smooth and elastic.
    4. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1-2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

    Prepare the Filling:

    1. In a bowl, combine the brown sugar and cinnamon. Add the melted butter and mix until it becomes a paste. Optionally, you can add chopped nuts or raisins to the filling.

    Assemble the Cake:

    1. Once the dough has risen, punch it down and roll it out into a large rectangle on a lightly floured surface.
    2. Spread the filling evenly over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges.
    3. Roll the dough up tightly, starting from one of the long edges, to form a log.
    4. Bring the ends of the log together to form a ring, pinching the edges together to seal.
    5. Place the ring on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rise again for about 30-45 minutes.

    Bake the Cake:

    1. Preheat your oven to 375째F (190째C).
    2. Bake the King Cake for 25-30 minutes, or until it is golden brown.
    3. Allow the cake to cool on a wire rack.

    Decorate the Cake:

    1. In a small bowl, mix the powdered sugar, milk, and vanilla extract to make the icing. It should be thick but pourable.
    2. Drizzle the icing over the cooled cake.
    3. Sprinkle the purple, green, and gold colored sugars over the icing in alternating sections.
    4. Optionally, hide the small plastic baby inside the cake from the bottom before serving.
